The complete guide to parking in Amsterdam

Amsterdam is the most expensive city for parking in the Netherlands and one of the priciest in Europe. In 2026, street parking in the centre costs €8.05 per hour — 24/7, no breaks on evenings or Sundays. A day trip can easily cost €50-80 just in parking.

Why P+R is the smartest option

For €1 per 24 hours, park at one of 10 P+R locations on the outskirts and ride metro, tram, or train in. You must check in/out with an OV-chipkaart. Return transport for two: ~€6-8. Total under €10 vs €50+ for a centre garage.

Use parking apps, not meters

EasyPark and Yellowbrick charge by the minute. Leave early and get money back — saves €2-5 per session vs rounding up at meters.

Enforcement is aggressive

Automatic scan cars check every plate. Fine: €82.00 plus parking owed. Foreign plates tracked too — see our parking fines in the Netherlands guide for appeal steps and what happens to foreign visitors. Always pay.

Seasonal Tips

Amsterdam parking by season

❄️

Winter (Nov-Feb)

Quietest period. P+R locations rarely fill. Even Sloterdijk has space by 10am. Garages are less crowded. Best time to drive if you must.

🌸

Spring (Mar-May)

Tulip season brings high volumes. P+R fills early on weekends — arrive before 09:00. King's Day (27 April): do not drive to Amsterdam.

☀️

Summer (Jun-Aug)

Peak tourist season. P+R Arena and Sloterdijk fill by 10:30 on weekends. Pre-book a garage or arrive before 09:00 for guaranteed P+R space.

🍂

Autumn (Sep-Oct)

ADE in October is the hardest period after King's Day. Otherwise manageable. P+R usually has space until 10:30 on weekdays.
