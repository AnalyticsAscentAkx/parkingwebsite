What is ParkBee?

ParkBee is a Dutch parking technology company that unlocks parking capacity in private and commercial garages across the Netherlands. Instead of these garages sitting empty, ParkBee makes them available to drivers through their app — often at prices well below traditional parking operators like Q-Park or APCOA.

The model is simple: building owners and parking operators have spare capacity. ParkBee connects drivers to that spare capacity through their platform, using smart access technology (digital keys, licence plate recognition) to open garages without needing a physical ticket or attendant.

Founded in Amsterdam, ParkBee has grown its network across the major Dutch cities. They operate in Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, The Hague (Den Haag), and Haarlem.

ParkBee's 4 parking products

ParkBee offers different products for different needs. Understanding which one fits your situation is key to getting the most value.

Pay Per Minute (PPM)

The most flexible option. You open the app, tap check-in, park, and tap check-out when you leave. You're charged by the minute — not rounded up to the nearest hour. For a 40-minute stay you pay for 40 minutes. For a 3-hour stay you pay for exactly 3 hours.

This is best for short or unpredictable stays. If you're visiting somewhere for 1-3 hours and aren't sure exactly how long you'll need, Pay Per Minute removes the anxiety of watching the clock. The rate is slightly higher per hour than Prepaid, but you never overpay.

Typical Amsterdam rate: ~€3.50-5.00/hr billed per minute.

Prepaid Parking

You book a parking spot in advance for a specific time window. Cheaper than Pay Per Minute, but you commit to a slot. Good if you know exactly when you'll arrive and roughly how long you'll stay.

Pre-booking also guarantees your spot — a big advantage at busy locations near stadiums, concert venues, or the airport. ParkBee's Prepaid option often shows availability at sold-out garages where walk-in is impossible.

Typical Amsterdam rate: ~€2.50-4.00/hr pre-booked.

Subscription

A monthly parking pass at a fixed location. Best for commuters who park at the same place every day. ParkBee subscriptions are typically 20-40% cheaper than direct monthly contracts with traditional operators for the same garages.

The trade-off: you're locked to one location and one garage. But for office workers who drive to work in Amsterdam, Utrecht, or Rotterdam, this can save €50-100 per month versus buying day tickets.

DigitalKey

A product used primarily by businesses and fleet operators. Instead of physical parking cards or per-vehicle accounts, drivers use a DigitalKey credential through the ParkBee platform for access. Useful for companies managing multiple vehicles across several locations.

When does ParkBee save you money?

ParkBee is typically cheaper than Q-Park, APCOA, and similar operators for the same garage — because ParkBee focuses on filling unused capacity rather than premium pricing. You'll often find ParkBee rates 15-30% below the on-site advertised rate for the same location.

The biggest savings come from:

  • Pre-booking popular garages: ParkBee often has access to spots that are "full" for walk-ins
  • Pay Per Minute for short stays: Avoid paying for time you didn't use
  • Monthly subscriptions: Much cheaper than day-rate parking for regular commuters
  • Event parking: Pre-book near stadiums and venues where street parking is impossible

ParkBee in Amsterdam

Amsterdam is ParkBee's strongest market. Their network covers key areas including garages near Amsterdam Olympisch Stadion, Ziggo Dome, Johan Cruyff Arena, Amsterdam RAI, and various locations near Amsterdam Centraal and the canal ring.

For event days (Ajax matches, concerts at Ziggo Dome, conferences at RAI), ParkBee pre-booking is often the only way to guarantee a spot within a reasonable distance. Regular day parking options fill up hours before events.

That said, for budget-conscious visitors without a specific event, the P+R system at €1/24hr is still cheaper than ParkBee for most use cases. ParkBee wins when you need a specific location, a guaranteed spot, or flexible in-and-out access.

Dynamic pricing — how it works

Like most modern parking operators, ParkBee uses dynamic pricing. Rates go up when demand is high (event days, peak hours) and down when garages have empty space. The price you see when you open the app is the current price for that moment.

Practical implications: checking the app in advance of an event will often show lower prices than on the day. If you're flexible on timing, searching the app a few days ahead often reveals pre-book discounts of 10-25%.
